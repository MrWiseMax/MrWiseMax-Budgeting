    Financial Education

    Tips, concepts, and resources to sharpen your financial knowledge.

    📐

    The 50/30/20 Rule

    Allocate 50% of income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings and debt. A simple, powerful framework that works for most income levels.

    🔥

    The FIRE Movement

    Financial Independence, Retire Early. Save and invest 40-70% of income aggressively to reach financial independence as soon as possible.

    🛡️

    Emergency Fund First

    Before investing, build 3-6 months of living expenses as a cash emergency fund. This protects you from needing to sell investments at a loss.

    📈

    Compound Interest

    Investing $200/month at 7% annual return becomes ~$240,000 in 30 years. Start early — time is the most powerful financial tool available.

    💳

    Debt Avalanche Method

    Pay minimum on all debts, then throw every extra dollar at the highest-interest debt first. Mathematically the fastest and cheapest way to become debt-free.

    🎯

    Pay Yourself First

    Automate your savings the moment your paycheck arrives. Treat savings like a non-negotiable bill. What's left is yours to spend freely.

    🏠

    Housing Rule of Thumb

    Keep housing costs (rent or mortgage) under 30% of your gross income. Above 30% limits your ability to save, invest, and build wealth.

    📊

    Net Worth Tracking

    Net worth = Assets − Liabilities. Track it monthly. Growing net worth means you're making progress — regardless of your income level.

    🌍

    Diversification

    Never put all savings into one asset, company, or account. Spread investments across different types to reduce risk without sacrificing returns.
